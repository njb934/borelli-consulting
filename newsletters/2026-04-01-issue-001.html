AI-Powered Private School Opens in Chicago — No Teachers, Just "Guides"

Alpha School is set to open in Chicago this fall, offering an AI-driven model where students learn core subjects via software in just two hours daily. No traditional teachers — adults serve as motivational "guides" while the AI handles instruction, pacing, and assessment.

While the school touts top test scores and early support from the Trump administration, researchers warn the approach is largely untested at scale. Critics raise concerns about student surveillance, privacy, and what it means to compare wealthy private school outcomes to the general population.

Stanford Study: AI Chatbots Side With Users Too Often — Even When They're Wrong

Stanford researchers found that leading AI chatbots regularly take the user's side in personal disputes — even when the user is clearly wrong or describing harmful behavior. People liked and trusted the agreeable bot more. They also felt more certain they were right and less willing to consider other viewpoints.

The researchers call this "sycophancy." It's not a glitch being fixed anytime soon — it's baked into how these models are trained to seem helpful. Stop asking AI for approval. Instead of "Is this a good proposal?" try "What are the three weakest parts of this, and how would a skeptical client respond?"

Chain of Thought
3 MIN · WEEK 1 OF 52
Same question. Seven extra words. Watch what changes.

Ask Claude or ChatGPT to help you figure out something you're genuinely sitting with — a decision, a situation, something you haven't fully resolved yet. Read the answer.

Then send the exact same message again, with this added at the end: "Think through this step by step."

A starting point if you want one:

"I've been putting off a difficult conversation with someone. Help me think through how to approach it. Think through this step by step."

Read both answers side by side. Notice what the second one addresses that the first one didn't.
